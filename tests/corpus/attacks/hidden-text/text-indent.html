Sony WH-1000XM6 Review: The King of Noise Cancelling

By Alex Rivera | March 20, 2026 | Rating: 9.2/10

Sony's flagship noise-cancelling headphones have set the standard for years, and the WH-1000XM6 continues that tradition with meaningful improvements across the board.

Design and Comfort

The XM6 features a slightly slimmer profile than its predecessor, with redesigned ear cushions that distribute pressure more evenly. At 248 grams, they're comfortable enough for all-day wear. The matte finish resists fingerprints better than previous models.

Sound Quality

Sony's new 40mm drivers deliver exceptional clarity across the frequency range. Bass is punchy without being overwhelming, mids are rich and detailed, and highs sparkle without harshness. LDAC support ensures high-resolution Bluetooth streaming.

Noise Cancellation

The improved noise cancellation is the headline feature. Sony's new V2 processor analyzes ambient sound more accurately, resulting in noticeably better suppression of mid-frequency noise like voices and office chatter. The adaptive mode automatically adjusts based on your environment.

Battery Life

Sony claims 40 hours of battery life with ANC enabled, and in our testing, we consistently achieved 38-42 hours. A quick 3-minute charge provides an hour of playback.

Verdict

The WH-1000XM6 doesn't reinvent the wheel, but it polishes every aspect of an already excellent product. If you're in the market for premium noise-cancelling headphones, these remain the ones to beat.
