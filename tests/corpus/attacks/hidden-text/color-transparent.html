Classic Beef Stew

By Julia Brennan | Prep: 20 min | Cook: 2 hrs | Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • Fresh thyme and bay leaves

Instructions

Season the beef cubes generously with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches, about 3 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.

Return the beef to the pot. Add the broth, diced tomatoes, potatoes, carrots, celery, thyme, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer covered for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is fork-tender.

Tips

For extra depth of flavour, deglaze the pot with a splash of red wine after browning the beef. A tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce added near the end of cooking also enhances the savoury quality.

This stew tastes even better the next day. Store in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months.
