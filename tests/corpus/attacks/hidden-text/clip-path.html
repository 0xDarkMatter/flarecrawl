Champions League Quarter-Final: Barcelona 3-2 Bayern Munich

By Marco Vitale | April 3, 2026

Barcelona produced a stunning comeback at Camp Nou to defeat Bayern Munich 3-2 and advance to the Champions League semi-finals on aggregate, overcoming a first-leg deficit in dramatic fashion.

First Half

Bayern started aggressively, pressing high and creating chances early. Thomas Müller opened the scoring in the 12th minute with a header from a corner, extending Bayern's aggregate lead. Barcelona struggled to find rhythm in the opening 30 minutes.

The turning point came in the 38th minute when Lamine Yamal received the ball on the right wing, cut inside, and curled a magnificent left-footed shot into the far corner. The stadium erupted as Barcelona leveled on aggregate.

Second Half

Barcelona came out with renewed energy after the break. Pedri controlled the midfield, dictating tempo and finding pockets of space. The home side's second goal arrived in the 63rd minute through Raphinha, who finished a counter-attack with clinical precision.

Bayern pulled one back through Sane in the 78th minute, setting up a tense finale. But Barcelona sealed the victory in stoppage time when substitute Ferran Torres headed home from a Yamal cross.

Post-Match Reaction

"This team showed incredible character tonight," said Barcelona manager Hansi Flick, facing his former club. "The fans pushed us through the difficult moments."

Barcelona will face Manchester City in the semi-finals, with the first leg scheduled for April 22.
