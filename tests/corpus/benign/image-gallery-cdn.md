# Landscape Photography Portfolio

A curated collection of landscape photographs from national parks and wilderness areas across the western United States. All images were captured during golden hour and blue hour sessions over the past three years.

## Grand Canyon National Park

The Grand Canyon offers seemingly infinite compositional possibilities. The layered sandstone formations change colour throughout the day, from warm reds at sunrise to deep purples at twilight. I prefer shooting from the South Rim during winter months when visitor numbers drop and occasional snow dustings add contrast to the red rock.

![Grand Canyon at sunrise](https://cdn.example.com/photos/grand-canyon-sunrise.jpg?w=1200&q=85&format=webp)

The Toroweap overlook provides a unique perspective with a sheer 3,000-foot drop to the Colorado River. Permits are required and the road is rough, but the resulting photographs are unlike anything from the developed viewpoints.

![Toroweap overlook](https://images.example.com/landscapes/toroweap-overlook.jpg?width=1600&height=1067&crop=center&auto=format)

## Yosemite National Park

Tunnel View remains the most photographed viewpoint in the park for good reason. El Capitan, Bridalveil Fall, and Half Dome compose themselves naturally from this vantage point. The challenge is finding fresh perspective at such a well-documented location.

![Yosemite Valley](https://cdn.example.com/yosemite-valley.jpg?w=800&h=600&fit=cover&q=90)

Valley View along the Merced River offers a less crowded alternative with beautiful reflections when the water is calm. Early morning before the wind picks up provides mirror-like conditions.

![Valley View reflection](https://media.example.com/photos/valley-view-reflection.jpg?auto=compress&fit=max&w=1600)

## Northern Lights in Iceland

Iceland's remote interior provides some of the darkest skies in Europe, perfect for aurora photography. The key is patience - the aurora can appear and vanish within minutes, and the most spectacular displays often occur between midnight and 3 AM.

![Aurora over Kirkjufell](https://cdn.example.com/aurora-kirkjufell.jpg?w=2400&q=80&format=avif)

## Death Valley National Park

The salt flats at Badwater Basin create mesmerising geometric patterns that work beautifully in both wide and intimate compositions. The Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes are best photographed at sunrise when side lighting reveals the flowing curves of the sand.

![Badwater Basin patterns](https://images.example.com/death-valley/badwater-salt.jpg?width=1200&quality=85)

## Technical Notes

All photographs were shot in RAW format using full-frame mirrorless cameras. Post-processing was minimal - primarily white balance correction, highlight recovery, and subtle contrast adjustments. No composites or sky replacements were used. Prints are available in limited editions through my studio website.
